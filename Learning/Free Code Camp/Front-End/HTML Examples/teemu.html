Teemu Selanne

"The Finnish Flash"

Teemu Selanne raising the Stanley Cup as a member of the Anaheim Ducks in 2007.

You keep shooting. You hope it goes in, and you smile.

Timeline throughout National Hockey League (NHL) Career:

  • 1988: At 17, selected in the first round with the 10th overall pick in the NHL entry draft by Winnipeg.
  • 1992-1993: Sets NHL rookie records for goals (76) and points (132) while winning the Calder Memorial Trophy as the league's top rookie.
  • 1996: Traded by Winnipeg along with Marc Chouinard and a fourth-round draft pick to the Ducks for Oleg Tverdovsky, Chad Kilger and a third-round pick.
  • 1998-1999: Wins the inaugural Maurice “Rocket” Richard Trophy as the league's top goal scorer, with 47.
  • 2001: Traded by the Ducks to San Jose for Jeff Friesen, Steve Shields and a second-round pick.
  • 2003: Signs as a free agent with Colorado.
  • 2005: Returns to Anaheim.
  • 2006: Scores his 1,000th career point (a goal) against the Kings.
  • 2006-2007: At 36, became the oldest player in NHL history to score as many as 45 goals in a season, finishing with 48.
  • 2007: Wins Stanley Cup with the Ducks, defeating Ottawa in five games.
  • 2010: Scores his 600th NHL goal, becoming the 18th player and third European in NHL history to do so.
  • 2013:
    • Had two goals and two assists against Vancouver to become the oldest NHL player in 42 years to score four points in a game. At 42 years and 200 days, became the oldest player to appear in a Ducks sweater.
    • Scored the 250th power-play goal of his career to move into sole possession of third place.
    • Moved past Luc Robitaille into 11th place on the all-time goals list by scoring the 669th of his career in a win over Minnesota.
    • Had a goal against Detroit to move past Bryan Trottier into 15th place on the all-time points list.
    • Played his 1,373rd game to surpass Teppo Numminen and became the leader in career NHL games by a Finnish-born player.
  • 2014: Retired from the NHL.
